Bryan,

I saw this posted and asked the same thing.  I tried the following:

.  I ran it through Mathematica's PrimeQ[n] test and it claimed the number
was prime.  Mathematica runs two passes of Miller-Rabin-Selfridge followed
by a single pass of the Lucas test (note, not Lucas-Lehmer).

2.  I ran it across Dario Alpern's ECM method and it claimed this was prime.
Also, it didn't find the factorization (of course, if it claimed it to be
prime).

3.  I ran this across my implementation of the exotic Lucas Primality test
and it claimed this was prime.

4.  I ran this across my implementation of MRS for the around 150 different
bases and it claimed it was a prime.

I think this number is prime and the poster might be a troll as he hasn't
produced the factorization he claims!

Thanks for double checking it and now I think he is wrong even more.  He
still hasn't produced said factorization!
